The Story of the 2017 New York Yankees

Gregory Csak

The 2017 season was a rollercoaster of emotions. During this time period the Yankees were not winning in previous years. Their last “successful” season was 2012. Going into the 2017 season the expectations were pretty low for this team. With veteran players retiring and some still on the team, and a mixture of young players who had yet to prove their full potential, many expected this team to not go very far. A few young players came onto the scene in 2016 such as pitcher Luis Severino, catcher Gary Sanchez, and outfielder Aaron Judge. Judge and Severino did not perform all that well in their brief time in the majors, however that was the opposite for Sanchez who came up to the big leagues in mid-August and went on to hit 20 home runs in 53 games and nearly won the Rookie of the Year award. But many were scratching their heads going into 2017 with many questions. Will Sanchez have another good year, or was 2016 a fluke? Will Judge and Severino make an impact on this team after last years struggles? The media and fans were anxious to know.

Moment #1

Image by Lenora Cagle from Pixabay
On Friday April 28th, the Yankees played the Baltimore Orioles in New York. During this time the Orioles were in first place in the AL East Division with the Yankees right behind them. In the 6th inning, the Orioles led the game by a score of 9-1, and it looked like this game was over. However, that was not the case, these young stars were never out of a game. Rookie Aaron Judge hit his second home run of the game to make the score 9-4, that home run came off of his bat at 119.4 mph. The Orioles scored two more runs and it was now 11-4 Baltimore in the 7th inning. With veteran Jacoby Ellsbury at the plate, the bases were loaded. Ellsbury launched his 100th career home run, which was a grand slam. The score was now 11-8 Orioles, and then everyone watching this game had that feeling that it was far from over. The score remained the same at 11-8 till the 9th inning, this was the last chance for the Yankees to make a comeback. They scored a run to make it 11-9, and with one runner on base second basemen Starlin Castro was at the plate. He crushed a game tying 2-run home run, miraculously down 9-1 in the 6th inning, the Yankees came back to tie the game. The game was all tied up at 11-11. The game went into extra innings, in the bottom of the 10th inning two runners were on base for veteran Matt Holliday. Holliday hit a game winning 3 run home run, after down 9-1, the Yankees won the game 14-11, and were now tied with the Orioles for first place. After this night, Yankees fans knew this team and this season was going to be something special.

Moment #2

Image by Gary Cassel from Pixabay
The Yankees were in Chicago to face the defending World Champions. The Cubs scored 1 run in the 1st inning and another in the 6th. It was 2-0 Cubs late in the game. Fast forward to the 9th inning, with the score still 2-0, the Cubs were right in the reach of victory. The Yankees put 2 men on base to show a glimpse of hope, and veteran Brett Gardner was up to bat. With 2 runners on, and 2 outs, the Yankees were 1 strike away from losing the game. Amazingly, down to their last strike Brett Gardner hit a go ahead 3-run home run, the Yankees were in the lead 3-2. On the edge of a loss once again with their backs against the wall, the Yankees had done it again. With two more games remaining against the Cubs, the Yankees won the next 2. They had won all 3 games against a team loaded with talent who had just won the World Series.

Moment #3

Image by krisr1 from Pixabay
The Yankees continued to win, and win, and win. On September 23rd against the Toronto Blue Jays, the Yankees officially put themselves into the 2017 postseason. Before the season started this team was not projected to make the playoffs, but they proved everyone wrong. The regular season ended on October 1st. In a 162-game season the Yankees finished with a record of 91-71. It was their first 90-win season since 2012 when they won 95 games. Going back to the young stars, Aaron Judge hit 52 home runs, at the time it was the most home runs in a season by a rookie. Gary Sanchez finished the year with 33 home runs, and pitcher Luis Severino finished the year with a 2.98 earned run average (ERA). After disappointing 2016 campaigns for Judge and Severino, they were fabulous in 2017. Aaron Judge nearly won the 2017 MVP award, but did take home the American League Rookie of the year award. Luis Severino also nearly won the Cy Young award. As for Gary Sanchez, he proved that his short but dominate 2016 season was not a one hit wonder. The Yankees had themselves a great young core of players, with more to come.

Moment #4

Image by Free-Photos from Pixabay
Going into the postseason there was lots excitement, the Yankees had to play a one game elimination game against the Minnesota Twins (Wild Card game). The winner of that game would advance to play the World Series favorite Cleveland Indians in a best of 5 American League Division Series (ALDS). The Yankees had their top young pitcher for the Wild Card game in Luis Severino, it seemed the Yankees would win this game with ease. The first batter of the game hit a home run; it was quickly 1-0 Twins. A few batters later the Twins had a 3-0 lead after a 2-run home run. Yankee fans were in shock, we thought our season was over 30 minutes into the first playoff game. Luis Severino was taken out of the game immediately, the Twins now seemed to be the favorite to win this do or die game. But the 2017 Yankees never quit, that was their energy all season. In the bottom of the 1st inning the Yankees quickly put 2 runners on base. Shortstop Didi Gregorius was batting representing the tying run. With fans on their feet hoping the seasons stays alive, Didi Gregorius hits a game tying 3-run Home Run, and the stadium went crazy. It was the biggest hit for the Yankees in about 5 years. The game was now tied 3-3. The Yankees went on to win the elimination game 8-4 and played the heavyweight Cleveland Indians in a best of 5 series.

Moment #5

Image by A.V. Flores from Pixabay
Going into the next round, the Indians were the clear favorite. They were the best team in baseball winning 102 games in the regular season. Cleveland won the first two games of the series; they were 1 win away from moving onto the American League Championship Series (ALCS). The series went back to NY, the Yankees had their backs against the wall. Game 3 was a must win. In game 3 the score was tied 0-0 till the bottom of the 7th, the Indians called on their best relief pitcher in Andrew Miller, and arguably one of the best pitchers in baseball at the time. Greg Bird was at the plate and hit a solo Home Run to give the Yankees the lead late in the game at 1-0. Their season had hope again. They won game 3, and game 4 was scheduled for tomorrow night. New York won game 4 and forced a winner take all game 5 to see who advances to the ALCS against the Houston Astros.

Moment #6

Image by Free-Photos from Pixabay
The Indians had their best starting pitcher in game 5 in Corey Kluber, who would go on to win the American League Cy young award. Didi Gregorius (the hero in the wild card game) homered off Kluber in the first inning. It was 1-0 Yankees. A few innings later Didi hit a two-run home run off him, just like that the Yankees lead the game 3-0. The score was then 3-2 until the top of the 9th inning. NY scored two more runs; it was now 5-2. The Yankees won the game and knocked out the heavyweight Indians. After down 0-2 to the World Series favorite, they had made another comeback winning three straight games.

Moment #7

Image by piviso from Pixabay
NY went on to face the Houston Astros in a best of 7 American League Championship Series (ALCS) to determine who would play in the World Series. The Astros were much better than the Yankees and were the clear favorite. NY lost the first two games in Houston, again down 0-2. With the series back in NY and their season on the line. The Yankees had to win just one game. NY won game 3 and Houston now led the series 2 games to 1. In a must win game 4 the Astros took a 4-0 lead late in the game. Many thought the Yankees could not comeback again. They were about to be down 3 games to 1 in a best of 7. In the 7th inning, Aaron Judge put the Yankees on the board with a solo home run. The score was now 4-1 Astros. NY continued to show life making it 4-2 going into the bottom of the 8th. In the 8th inning, 6 outs away from a loss. Miraculously down 4-0 with their season on the line again. The Yankees came back to tie it 4-4. Later on, in the 8th inning with two runners on base and Gary Sanchez at the plate he lined a two-run double to give the Yankees the lead over the Astros 6-4. NY won game 4, and game 5 was the next day at Yankee Stadium with the series tied 2-2. The Yankees won game 5 and lead the series over the Astros 3-2. After being a team that was projected to not make the playoffs, a team that fell behind in the Wild Card game, a team that was down 0-2 and knocked out the Indians, and down 0-2 again. The Yankees were just 1 win away from a World Series, just 1 more win till their first World Series appearance since 2009. The series went back to Houston with NY leading the series 3-2.

Moment #8

Image by Sue Park from Pixabay
With the Yankees leading the series 3-2 and just one win away from the World Series, the Astros won game 6 forcing a winner take all game 7 to decide who will play the Los Angeles Dodgers in the 2017 World Series. Unfortunately, after a magical season, and countless comebacks with their season on the line when many doubted, they could not do it. The Yankees lost game 7, the Astros won the series 4 games to 3 and played the Dodgers in the World Series. Ultimately, the Houston Astros won the World Series, their first championship in franchise history. The magical 2017 season for the Yankees came to a close, but it was a season many fans will forever be thankful for and one nobody will ever forget.
